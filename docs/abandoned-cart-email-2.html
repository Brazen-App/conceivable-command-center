CoQ10 (Ubiquinol)

Here's something most women don't know: egg quality isn't fixed. Your eggs need enormous amounts of energy to mature properly — more energy than almost any other cell in your body. CoQ10 is the fuel your mitochondria use to produce that energy.

A 2018 study in the journal Fertility and Sterility showed that CoQ10 supplementation improved ovarian response and embryo quality in women undergoing IVF. Another study found that women who supplemented with CoQ10 for 60 days had significantly improved egg quality markers. But here's the catch: dose matters enormously. Most over-the-counter supplements contain 100-200mg. The therapeutic dose used in fertility research is 400-600mg of the bioavailable form (ubiquinol, not ubiquinone — yes, there's a difference, and yes, it matters).

NAC (N-Acetyl Cysteine)

NAC is one of the most underrated supplements in fertility. It's a precursor to glutathione — your body's master antioxidant — and it plays a critical role in reducing oxidative stress, which is one of the primary drivers of poor egg quality and implantation failure.

A landmark study published in Reproductive BioMedicine Online found that NAC supplementation significantly improved ovulation rates in women with PCOS. But it's not just for PCOS — NAC supports liver detoxification, reduces systemic inflammation, and helps regulate insulin sensitivity. In our pilot data across 105 women, those on a protocol including NAC saw measurable improvements in inflammatory markers within 60 days. For anyone dealing with unexplained infertility, chronic inflammation is almost always part of the picture.

Omega-3 Fatty Acids (EPA/DHA)

Omega-3s aren't new or sexy, and that's exactly why they get overlooked. But the research on omega-3s and fertility is genuinely compelling.

A Harvard study following over 900 women found that higher omega-3 intake was associated with a significantly higher probability of natural conception. DHA in particular is critical for hormone production — your body literally uses it to build progesterone. And the EPA component is one of the most effective natural anti-inflammatories we know of. But here's what most people get wrong: the ratio of EPA to DHA matters, and most cheap fish oil supplements are heavy on EPA and light on DHA. For fertility, you want a balanced or DHA-dominant formula. (The stuff in your pack? Already optimized for this.)
